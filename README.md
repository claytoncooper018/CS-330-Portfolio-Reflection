# CS 330 – Computational Graphics Portfolio Reflection

## How do I approach designing software?
When I develop software, I start by organizing the project's general structure before segmenting it into smaller parts. I concentrated on the placement of things in the 3D world and how users would interact with them for this project. Before starting implementation, I also took object relationships, lighting, and visual layout into account.

## What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
My spatial awareness in 3D environments and my comprehension of how textures, lighting, and transformations impact visual output have both improved as a result of this project. Additionally, I became more adept at organizing scenes before to coding.

## What design process did you follow for your project work?
I used an iterative design methodology. I started with a basic scene and added items, textures, and lighting one at a time. I tested and modified the design after every stage to increase usability and realism.

## How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
Any software development process can benefit from breaking projects up into smaller components and iterating over time. I'll keep using the tactics of planning before coding and improving through testing.

## How do I approach developing programs?
I write organized, modular code and conduct frequent testing as part of my development methodology. I concentrate on developing one feature at a time and making sure it functions before further.

## What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
I made use of texture mapping, shader management, and organized rendering approaches. Additionally, I became more adept at diagnosing graphics-related problems like lighting and object rendering.

## How did iteration factor into your development?
Iteration was crucial. Until the scene appeared right, I kept testing the program, moving objects, enhancing textures, and fine-tuning the lighting.

## How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?
I grew more at ease with organizing and troubleshooting code over time. I also learned how to build cleaner, more effective code and foresee problems before they arise.

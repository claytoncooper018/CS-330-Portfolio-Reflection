# CS 330 – Computational Graphics Portfolio Reflection

## How do I approach designing software?
I approach designing software by first planning the overall structure and breaking the project into smaller components. For this project, I focused on how objects would be placed in the 3D scene and how users would interact with them. I also considered visual layout, lighting, and object relationships before beginning implementation.

## What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
This project helped me develop spatial awareness in 3D environments, as well as a better understanding of how textures, lighting, and transformations affect visual output. I also improved my ability to plan scenes before coding.

## What design process did you follow for your project work?
I followed an iterative design process. I began with a simple scene, then gradually added objects, textures, and lighting. After each step, I tested and adjusted the design to improve realism and usability.

## How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
Breaking projects into smaller parts and iterating over time can be applied to any software development process. Planning before coding and refining through testing are strategies I will continue to use.

## How do I approach developing programs?
I approach development by writing modular, organized code and testing frequently. I focus on building one feature at a time and ensuring it works before moving forward.

## What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
I used structured rendering techniques, shader management, and texture mapping. I also improved debugging skills when working with graphics-related issues like lighting and object rendering.

## How did iteration factor into your development?
Iteration was essential. I repeatedly tested the program, adjusted object placement, improved textures, and refined lighting until the scene looked correct.

## How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?
Over time, I became more comfortable with debugging and organizing code. I also learned to anticipate issues earlier and write cleaner, more efficient code.
